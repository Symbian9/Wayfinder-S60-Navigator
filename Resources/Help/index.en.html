Navigation help



Main menu


You can find and set your destination by making a search, by using one of your saved favourite places or by selecting a place on the map.

Searches can be made for addresses, companies, people*, phone numbers*, interesting places and much more. You can save any search results and map positions as Favourites.

Several menu options are available. You can set or change the password you use to log into http://maps.vodafone.com. Select the option My account to find your username and set or change your password.

* Not available in all countries and regions.



Navigation map


The Navigation map view displays your route in a map view, either in 3D or in 2D. Your route is displayed as a coloured line on the map, with your position indicated by an icon.

The larger icon at the top left shows the next event or turn in your route. The current street name is displayed at the bottom of the screen, and the next street name at the top of the screen.



Guide


In the Guide view you will see and hear turn-by-turn instructions for your calculated route. On the top of the screen the bigger icon shows the next event or turn in your route and the distance to it. The smaller icon shows the next event or turn after that.

Hint: The Guide view requires only a small amount of data traffic while navigating.



Itinerary


The Itinerary view shows a list of all turns to the destination. The current turn is indicated in the list (if your phone is receiving GPS signal).



Search


You can find addresses, companies, people*, phone numbers* and interesting places by making a search. Just enter what you are searching for in the Search for field. You could also make a search for a category, e.g. airport, ATM, hotel or restaurant. To do this, choose the Select category option in the menu (or by tapping the striped bottom right icon if you have a touch screen phone). If you want to find something in the nearby area, choose My current position in the menu (or tap the icon if you have a touch screen phone)**.

To set another place or area to search in, enter the name of a city or an area in the in or close to field, e.g. Berlin or New York. Then enter the country or state you want to make the search within by clicking the Country/State field.

Perform the search by clicking Search in the menu (or tap the Search key if you have a touchscreen phone).

* Not available in all countries and regions.

**My current position is only visible when you have a position either from GPS or from the network. The GPS icon will turn green when you have a signal. Tap this key to enable or disable a search around your current position.



Search results


When a search has been performed you can select what kind of search results you are interested in, e.g. addresses, places, yellow and white pages.

If you cannot find the desired search result here, change your search term and please try again.

Once you have found what you are looking for, select it and start to navigate. You can also show it on the map or save it as a favourite.



Favourites


Find your saved favourite locations here. Your saved favourites can be either saved search results or locations saved from the map. Some phone models will also display landmarks saved from other applications.



Map


In the Map view certain points of interest (e.g. petrol stations and train stations) are displayed as icons. Your saved favourites are also displayed in the map.

Your position is indicated by an icon when you are receiving GPS signals. If you do not have a GPS signal, a circle in the map will indicate your approximate position.

If you have a phone without a touch screen, zoom in and out of the map by pressing 3 and 1 on your keypad. You can also scroll the map by pressing the joystick left, up, right and down.

On a touchscreen phone you tap the + and - to zoom in and out, and drag the map to move it around.

From the menu you can navigate to a position on the map, or save it as a favourite. If you have a touchscreen phone you tap and hold on a position in the map to bring up the map options. Select Save as favourite.



Settings


In the Settings view you can customise the application according to your own preferences.


General tab


The General tab contains general settings for the application, for example if you want to hear the complete set of voice instructions while you are navigating and if the application should connect to GPRS Internet only when needed (select "off"), or if the connection should be kept alive to improve connection speed (select "on").


Route tab


The Route tab contains settings that allow you to set e.g. if you want to optimise your route calculation to the fastest or shortest route available or turn automatic reroute on or off.


Map tab


In the Map tab you select which POI categories to automatically download. You also select if you want the POIs to be minimised or normal.
